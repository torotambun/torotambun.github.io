I am Executive Director of Yayasan Mens et Manus, a Jakarta-based foundation committed to unlocking Indonesia's regional economic potential through innovation and entrepreneurship. I am also Founder and Director of Aren Energy Investment Pte Ltd, a corporate-backed, university-based accelerator in Singapore.

I completed my Doctor of Philosophy in Science in Management at Institut Teknologi Bandung (2023–2026) — Summa Cum Laude, GPA 4.0 of 4.0, Minimum Required Time for Graduation, Best Doctoral Student 2024 Award, and Scholarly Achievement Award SBM-ITB April 2026. My doctoral theory — the Dynamic Feedback Theory of Entrepreneurial Formation in Loss-Averse, Short-Horizon Cultures — explains why innovation ecosystems in Indonesia fail not from resource scarcity, but from suppressed feedback loops, cultural risk-aversion, and institutional conformity.

I bring over 24 years of executive experience in the oil and gas sector, including assignments across 52 countries. Thirteen of those years were spent with Schlumberger, where I held regional and global roles spanning operations, technology deployment, and organizational leadership.

I am an Industrial Lecturer at STEI-ITB (course EL-4244, Technology-Based Entrepreneurship, 2022–present) and an NIDK Lecturer at the Faculty of Agriculture, Universitas Gadjah Mada (Communication Course and Entrepreneurship Course, Odd Semester 2023/2024–Even Semester 2025/2026). I am a member of the Harvard Business Review Advisory Council and an MIT En-Roads Climate Simulation Ambassador.

I am available for board advisory, research collaboration, and speaking engagements. I am Manadonese — from North Sulawesi, Indonesia. I am based across Jakarta, Singapore, and Bandung.
